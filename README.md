# EngHack-Project
Problem Statement: 
In winter, seasonal affective disorder (SAD) is a common mental health issue. It is a type of depression with symptoms such as persistent sadness, hopelessness, and anxiety. [1] In Canada, about 15% of people experience mild SAD during their lifetime, while 2–3% experience severe cases. [2] Research shows that reduced sunlight exposure during winter is one of the primary causes of SAD because it disrupts circadian rhythms, along with cold weather and reduced outdoor activity [3][4] If untreated, SAD can significantly affect daily functioning and, in severe cases, result in “suicidal thoughts are behaviours”, highlighting the importance of early monitoring and intervention. Brainstorming: 
We came up with the solution by identifying common causes of seasonal affective disorder and found that insufficient light and unsuitable indoor temperature can contribute to depression and anxiety during winter. Thus, we designed a system that automatically adjusts these conditions to help prevent this from happening. We also recognize that improving a room’s environment may involve more than just temperature and light, so the system provides personalized recommendations based on different combinations of these factors.
During brainstorming, we also considered several other ideas: (1) anti-tripping systems, (2) snow depth and obstacle detection, and (3) a machine to electronically measure human body temperature. We rejected the first two ideas because they would require installation on crutches and might duplicate existing functions. The third idea was dismissed due to concerns about uncertainty and limited experimental data.
Solution:
Our current solution scans the entire window, performs data analysis, and checks whether the lighting and temperature of the room are sufficient to reduce the risk of seasonal affective disorder, particularly for elders and disabled individuals when a caretaker is not present. The product rotates 360 degrees to continuously record light and temperature levels. Based on the recorded data, it performs analysis using advanced statistical models like time-series analysis and multivariate regression analysis, AI, and big data. It then automatically adjusts the lighting and provides feedback on how to improve the local environment. 
Furthermore, our solution incorporates several customized settings to ensure users of different ages and health conditions can use the system easily. For example, we include accessibility features such as large text, support for WiFi-less homes, and high-contrast displays. We also recognize that the definition of a “suitable” environment may vary between individuals. Therefore, users can press a button to immediately adjust temperature and lighting, and the system will incorporate this preference into its analysis.
In the future, we plan to improve its accessibility and integration. Additional features may include a voice function that reads LCD text and automatic temperature adjustment. We also aim to add a mobile base with object detection so the device can collect data across an entire building or apartment. Finally, we plan to further develop the IoT capabilities of the system, which automatically applied those room settings so that people like elders don’t have to move to make those changes.
